Interactive Earthquake Map: U.S. Activity from April to May 2025 Powered by observablehq.com

Head to Interactive Map

This interactive map visualizes earthquake activity across the United States from April 4 to May 4, 2025. Each earthquake is represented as a proportional symbol, where the size of the circle reflects the event’s magnitude—scaled to emphasize the exponential nature of seismic energy release. Larger circles indicate stronger earthquakes, providing an immediate visual cue of their relative intensity.

The color of each symbol represents the event’s significance (denoted as "sig" in the dataset), a measure often used to reflect how newsworthy or impactful an earthquake may be based on factors like felt reports, magnitude, and location. Users can choose from four color schemes—reds, greens, blues, and purples—and apply one of three classification methods (Quantile, Equal Interval, or Natural Breaks) to the significance values. This allows for flexible interpretation depending on the desired analytical lens.

By default, the map displays Week 1 (April 4–10). Users can use the timeline slider to explore Weeks 2 through 4 or activate the “Show all weeks” checkbox to view cumulative activity over the entire month. This feature supports both focused temporal analysis and a broader overview of earthquake patterns throughout the study period.

Interactive dropdown menus and legends are embedded within the map to help users adjust classification and color settings with ease. The combined use of symbol size, color, classification controls, and temporal filtering enables deeper exploration and encourages users to interpret the spatial distribution of seismic events in a dynamic and informative way.
